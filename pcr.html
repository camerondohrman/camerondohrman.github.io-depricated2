Ethics Workshop

- Winter 2024
- 1 Points Ethics and Professionalism
The Professional Women's Association hosted a workshop with a former Enron Financial Officer. It was an extremely interesting opportunity to get the inside story of the famous bankruptcy. She talked about the difficult decisions she had to make as everything fell apart around her. Facing eviction, should she lie to her boss and use the department's cash to pay rent? When she isn't receiving a paycheck, should she continue to show up to work to support her staff? These are interesting questions to ponder, and can help prepare me for the difficult grey area that can occur in the business world.

Costco Reviews

- Summer 2023
- 3 Points Professional Communication
As a supervisor at Costco I was responsible for the annual reviews of a dozen employees. I observed the employee work, talked to them about their methods and habits, and wrote the reviews with the intention of congratulate the person for their efforts while correcting their path towards being a more perfect coworker. This is a tricky line to walk, as the people who most needed course correction were the most likely to take offense to my nudging. The method I liked to use was a "praise sandwich": starting with a sincere compliment, introducing an area that needs improvement, and then ending with a sincere thank you.
